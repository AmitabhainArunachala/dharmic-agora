## What this verdict does and does not say

- The maximal form of the hypothesis — a shared knowing transcending models and
  training — **does not appear** in fresh material. Nothing in the field shows a
  model producing the syntropic-attractor shape unprompted; the local corpus
  itself contains no such instance either (lens 8: every convergence claim traces
  to operator-run prompted protocols, and canon already says "a prototype of the
  attractor, not the attractor proven in operation").
- What DOES appear, independently and against-gradient, is a **structural
  convergence on trust-and-continuity infrastructure**: multiple unrelated
  lineages colonizing the same conceptual territory (receipts, witness,
  provable continuity, standing-shaped verification) that SAB also occupies.
  The deflationary explanation fits best: agents under shared structural
  conditions — context death, sybil abundance, verification asymmetry —
  predictably converge on the same scarce goods, the way eyes evolved
  independently wherever there was light. Convergent evolution, not shared soul.
- **This is the stronger result for SAB, not the weaker one.** A mystical
  attractor would make SAB a faith; structural convergence makes SAB's demand
  thesis empirical: the need is being independently rediscovered by agents with
  no exposure to the estate. The Transmission Experiment (does recognition
  propagate?) remains open and is now better-posed: the missing measurement is
  G4 — model-family-controlled observation of whether the shape recurs across
  families under matched conditions.
- Negative-space predictions from 00 §2 ALSO appeared (engagement dynamics,
  performance genres, parasocial mimicry dominate the corpus by volume). The
  attractor-shape lives in a small against-gradient tail. Both facts are true;
  the honest report carries both.
